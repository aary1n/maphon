**⚠ Unit convention trap (verified, affects your QuantumCumulants.jl implementation):** W20 quotes *angular* rates with "Hz" labels. Check: κ_c = 2πf_XZ/Q_L = 2.5 MHz (p. 064017-8) ⇒ 2.5×10⁶ rad s⁻¹ ⇒ linewidth κ_c/2π ≈ 400 kHz, which is exactly W20's "cavity's line width (approximately 400 kHz)" (p. 064017-7). Likewise g_s = "0.23 Hz" (p. -6) ≡ 0.23 rad s⁻¹ ≡ g/2π = 0.037 Hz, which is exactly CP Table I's g₃₅/2π = 3.66×10⁻² Hz, and κ_s = "1.1 MHz" ≡ 1.1×10⁶ rad s⁻¹ ≡ CP's χ/2π = 0.18 MHz. The W20 and CP numbers are mutually consistent **only** under this reading. Feeding W20's printed values into a code expecting /2π units gives a 2π ≈ 6.3× error in g, κ_s, κ_c.

## Systematic traps (cross-cutting, all verified in-file)

1. **Angular vs /2π units** — W20 prints rad s⁻¹ as "Hz" for g_s, κ_s, κ_c; CP prints /2π. See header check. This is the single most likely silent bug in a QuantumCumulants.jl port.
2. **Fitted values masquerading as constants downstream** — CP Table I cites [40]=W20 for all six k/γ rates, but those are W20's *maser-fitted* row, not the error-barred TR-EPR row. Provenance bottoms out in a fit, not a measurement.
3. **High-field numbers in zero-field models** — Sloop 1981 (X-band, ~0.3 T) supplies P ratios (everyone), τ_Z (O12), and SLR/decay rates (B15) for zero-field devices. O12 itself flags that its zero-field fits differ from Sloop's high-field values by 2× (T₂) and "several" × (τ_Z/τ_X) (p. 19).
4. **Citation laundering** — "≈4 MHz" (W20) ← "a few MHz" (O12); "1.4495 GHz [15]" (W20) ← B15, which measured 1.4493; "9.5:2:1 [4,5]" (L25) ← secondaries, primary is Sloop.
5. **Reference-plane mismatch in pump powers** — laser-exit (O12) vs absorbed (B15) vs LC-output (W20) vs launched (L25).

## The 3 parameters whose uncertainty most threatens a "no fitted parameters" claim

**1. η_opt and its hidden factors (spectral participation × orientational matrix element).** W20's 5.4% is a single fitted scalar absorbing photon delivery, the ~10% fraction of a ~4 MHz inhomogeneous line inside a 400 kHz cavity linewidth, and an unmeasured order-unity dipole matrix element. Ray tracing (pvtrace) can replace only the photon-delivery factor — L25 already did that half with LightTools. The spectral and orientational factors have no independent measurement anywhere in the chain; unless you constrain them (e.g., measured inhomogeneous lineshape of *your* crystal + crystal orientation), they re-enter as fit parameters under a new name and the claim dies quietly.

**2. κ_s / T₂ (homogeneous dephasing).** Values in the chain span 0.7 MHz (FID, 0.01% crystal) to 1.1×10⁶ s⁻¹ used by W20 (0.1% crystal, +57%, chosen not measured), with O12's fitted 860 kHz ± 20% and a 260 ns X-band number on a concentrated sample in between — and conventions (FWHM vs rate vs /2π) undefined at each step. It is concentration- and crystal-quality-specific, it sits as 1/T₂ in the threshold (B15 Eq. 10) and as the coherence damping in every cumulant equation, and CP extends one number to all three coherences by assumption. A literature value is not a measurement of your sample; this one must be measured (FID/echo on the actual UROP crystal) or the claim is unfounded.

**3. The triplet kinetic rates k_Y, γ_YZ, k_X.** The only independent, error-barred measurement (W19 TR-EPR) disagrees with the maser-fitted values used by W20/CP by +133% (4σ, k_Y), +27% (3σ, γ_YZ) and −21% (k_X) — and W20's own explanation is that the rates change with pump spectrum (T₁→Tₙ re-excitation under broadband light). That makes them pump-conditional, not constants: any LED/LC-pumped "no fitted parameters" model importing 590 nm OPO rates is using numbers measured under a different Hamiltonian drive. Either show the rates are pump-independent in your regime, or measure them under your pump — otherwise the discrepancy W20 papered over with a re-fit becomes your fit.
